Cult of the Lamb

•

2 Apr 2023

Have we finally found the people's publisher — Devolver Digital? As every new game from under their wings flies farther and higher, I can only hope this is yet one of many peaks to come. And yes, maybe spending all day today with Cult of the Lamb has me speaking idiomatically as a cult leader and civilization forger. After all, what else is the lamb of the most powerful devil this side of indie roguelike lore to do?

Here's what we have: exceptional music direction, pinpoint polished cartoon-but-not-Flash artstyle, and a Goldilocks unlock curve for abilities and worldbuilding. On top of that, we have a cute and surprisingly complex routine gameplay loops — think Animal Crossing, from hell.

Across the Obelisk

•

1 Apr 2023

I was drawn into this Slay the Spire-like deckbuilding roguelike when I read it supported co-op. From the first moment, two things became clear — this game does not look good in 4k, and this game feels ripped right out of the AdventureQuest universe.

After one battle, I was brought into the "Town," which reminded me of a point-and-click adventure. A shop, an upgrade station, plot points... this is richer, with more to customize and change my experience, than a single floor in the archetypal Slay the Spire or Dicey Dungeons.

Ultimately, I favor the art and focus of the classics, but should return to this when wishing for a story and online co-op teamwork.
